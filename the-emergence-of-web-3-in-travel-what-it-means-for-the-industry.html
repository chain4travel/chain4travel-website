The Emergence of Web 3.0 in Travel: What it Means for the Industry

The travel industry has been affected by the emergence and continued development of the internet. What Web 3.0 will mean for travel.

While reading this article, you’re using Web 2.0.
Soon enough, you’ll be using Web 3.0 when spending time online.

Maybe it’s not as obvious to you because you’re thinking that you’re “just using the internet” - but let us tell you: the internet has come a long way and so has its implications on the travel industry.

With this article we want to bring light into the darkness: What exactly do we mean by the often used term Web 3.0? What impact have the developments of the internet had on the travel industry? And finally - how can companies operating in the travel sector benefit from future innovations?

Main Takeaways

  1. Web 3.0 is based on the core concepts of decentralization, openness, data-security and greater benefits for users - all facilitated through the blockchain technology. As such, it could potentially be more disruptive and even a bigger paradigm shift as Web 2.0.
  2. Companies in the travel industry need to be aware that we are in the midst of an immense transformation. To stay relevant in the market, it is critical to actively shape this new way of thinking in the industry.
  3. Besides that, Web 3.0 offers industry players various possibilities to benefit from the new technologies : Traveler rewards, loyalty programs, more efficient processes, payment and settlement solutions and many more are products and techniques we can already start using today.
